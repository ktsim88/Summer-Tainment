Inca Trail to Machu Picchu

Welcome to my review of the Inca Trail in Peru.

I plan to mention about:

  • My experience on the hike
  • The People and Porters
  • The Food


The Actual Hike

I did the 5 days and 4 nights version of the trail. My family did this hike through a company called "Alpaca Expeditions" which is the largest company that does tours in the Inca Trail and other places like "Rainbow Mountain"

The altitude is 13,799 feet which could often leave people dropping out of the hike due to altitude sickness.

People

My Group

I loved the group that I was in. They were all very nice and there were no showoffs. We all became friends in the end after this hike.

One person from my group actually dropped out on the first day because of the altitude. Another person had an injured knee.
We missed some of the tour because we were a bit too slow.

The men in green are the Porters.
They help out with:

  • Carrying your belongings with their stuff
  • Setting up your tents
  • Cooking the food
They all range from a 20 year old to a 61 year old in my group.


Food

The food was delicious and very nutritious.

The food was cooked by the head chef. One person was vegan and another one was vegetarian. The chef would accomodate them with those needs.
